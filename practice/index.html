Practice by Difficulty

Every difficulty level changes the length of words and complexity of sentences you're asked to type. Start where you're comfortable and move up as your accuracy stays above 95%.

🌱 Beginner

Short, common words of 2-4 letters with simple, predictable letter patterns. Ideal if you're just learning where the keys are or building muscle memory for the home row.

Start

🙂 Easy

Everyday words of 4-6 letters and short, plain sentences. A comfortable step up once beginner words feel effortless and your typing speed is starting to climb.

Start

⚙ Medium

Mixed-length vocabulary, punctuation, and full sentences with natural rhythm. This is the default difficulty and a good general benchmark for your real-world WPM.

Start

🔥 Hard

Longer words, less common vocabulary, and denser sentence structure, including numbers and mixed capitalization. Pushes your accuracy under more finger-travel pressure.

Start

🏆 Expert

Technical and specialized vocabulary, long paragraphs, and irregular punctuation drawn from academic, business, and programming text. Built for typists chasing 80+ WPM.

Random Practice

🎲

Always Fresh Content

Every test already randomizes its content automatically. Words are drawn from shuffled tiers, and sentences and paragraphs use a no-repeat system that avoids showing you the same text twice until the full pool has been used. There's no separate "random mode" to turn on — just restart (press Tab) and you'll get new material every time.
